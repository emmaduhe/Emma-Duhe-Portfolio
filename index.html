Featured Works

TINY CHEF

Tiny Chef helps kids grow through hands-on playsets that build important skills in a natural, engaging way. Each set creates a familiar kitchen-inspired environment where children can explore and learn through experience rather than instruction. The design uses soft, rounded shapes to make tools feel approachable, along with bright, food-inspired colors to clearly distinguish different elements. Simple layouts and clear visual cues guide kids through activities without overwhelming them, while playful illustrations and slightly exaggerated forms add personality and keep the experience fun. These design choices support fine motor skills, problem-solving, and independence while keeping the focus on curiosity and open-ended play.

TRAVEL POSTER

This Hawaii travel poster explores a simplified, graphic interpretation of the islands through bold color and shape. The composition focuses on key visual cues like movement, landscape silhouettes, and warm colors to suggest place without relying on heavy detail. A restrained palette of saturated blues, greens, and sun-washed tones helps establish atmosphere while keeping the layout visually clear.

MOVIE POSTER

This Don’t Worry Darling movie poster places the main character at the center of the composition, making her the main focal point while surrounding visuals help develop the film’s world. Dancers, an eye motif, and a uniform suburban setting are layered throughout to reference key themes and moments. The design is paired with a matching popcorn bucket and drink cup, extending the visual identity beyond the poster itself. Together, the elements create a cohesive set that ties the promotional materials into one unified concept.
